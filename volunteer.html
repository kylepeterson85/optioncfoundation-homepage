Why It Matters

Your Experience Has Real Value — Especially for Someone Starting From Scratch

Many Option C participants are building their first business without a professional network, without a support system, and without anyone in their corner who has actually run a business. Your experience fills that gap.

Real-World Perspective

Most participants don't have access to anyone who's actually built and run a business. Your experience with real-world decisions, obstacles, and growth provides something no class or guide can replace.

Accountability Changes Outcomes

The biggest reason new businesses stall is the absence of someone to check in with. A mentor who asks "how did it go?" and "what's your next step?" can make the difference between a business that launches and one that stays an idea.

Lasting Impact

A successful small business built by an Option C participant doesn't just change their life — it can create stability for their family, contribute to their community, and demonstrate what's possible for others in similar situations.

The Role

What Mentors Do

Mentoring with Option C Foundation is structured, purposeful, and designed to fit around your schedule.

Regular Check-Ins

Meet virtually with your assigned participant on a regular schedule — typically every 2 to 4 weeks. These sessions are focused on progress, obstacles, and next steps.

Business Plan Review

Provide feedback on the participant's business plan — not as a formal review, but as someone who has built something and can spot gaps, ask good questions, and help refine thinking.

Practical Guidance

Share what you've learned the hard way — about customers, operations, pricing, marketing, cash flow, or whatever is most relevant to the participant's specific business type and situation.

Encouragement and Honesty

Good mentorship balances realistic feedback with genuine encouragement. Participants need someone who believes they can do it — and who will also be honest when something isn't working.

What We Look For

Who Makes a Good Mentor

You don't need to be a nationally recognized entrepreneur. You need to have built something, learned something, and be willing to share it honestly.

✓

Business Experience

You've started, built, or managed a business of any size — a solo service business, a small company, a side business you ran for years. The size doesn't matter. The experience does.

✓

Genuine Interest in Helping

You're interested in the success of someone who has faced real challenges — not as a project, but as a person who is trying to build something legitimate and meaningful.

✓

Reliability

You'll show up when you say you will, follow through on what you commit to, and be consistent with your participant — even when the schedule gets busy.

✓

Non-Judgmental Approach

Option C participants come from a wide range of backgrounds, including disability, prior incarceration, and long-term unemployment. We ask mentors to focus on where participants are going, not where they've been.

✓

Comfortable With Virtual Format

All mentoring is conducted remotely via video call. You need to be comfortable using basic video conferencing tools like Zoom, Google Meet, or similar.

✓

Time Commitment

Typically 2–4 hours per month — one scheduled check-in plus time for reviewing any materials or questions. The commitment is meaningful but designed to be manageable alongside a full-time career.
