Why Option C Exists

Many Capable People Are Denied a Fair Chance at Employment

For people with disabilities, prior felony convictions, long-term unemployment, or limited access to opportunity, the traditional job market can feel closed before they ever get a fair interview. Option C Foundation exists to provide another path: entrepreneurship. We help people build lawful, practical, and realistic small businesses that can create income, independence, and dignity.

Employment Barriers

Many people are rejected because of background checks, disability-related limitations, or gaps in work history — before they ever get to show what they can do.

Limited Support

Business ownership can be life-changing, but many people lack mentorship, structure, and startup guidance — especially those starting from scratch without a network.

A Better Path Forward

Option C helps participants turn business ideas into structured plans with real-world support — creating a realistic path to income, independence, and dignity.

Who We Serve

Entrepreneurship Support for People Facing Real Barriers

Option C Foundation focuses on adults who are motivated to build a better future but face barriers that make traditional employment difficult.

People With Disabilities

Flexible self-employment can help individuals build around their strengths, schedule, health needs, and abilities — without the rigid demands of traditional employment.

People With Prior Felony Convictions

We support individuals ready to move forward by helping them build legitimate businesses and long-term stability — focusing on potential, not just the past.

Long-Term Unemployed Individuals

For people who have faced repeated rejection, business ownership can create another way to generate income and reclaim their professional identity.

Aspiring Entrepreneurs Without Resources

Many people have solid ideas but lack training, mentorship, structure, and guidance. Option C helps close that gap with free, structured support.

Program Support

Free Business Launch Support for Qualified Participants

Accepted participants receive structured entrepreneurship support designed to help them move from idea to action.

01

Business Idea Validation

We help participants clarify their business idea, target audience, and realistic path forward — so they're building something worth pursuing.

02

Business Plan Support

Participants receive guidance creating a practical business plan they can understand, use, and build upon as their business grows.

03

Mentorship

Participants are connected with experienced mentors who provide accountability, direction, and real-world perspective throughout the process.

04

Business Setup Guidance

We help participants understand basic steps such as legal structure, digital presence, and operational planning to start on the right foot.

05

Website & Digital Presence

Participants receive guidance on building an online presence that helps their business look legitimate, trustworthy, and easy to find.

06

Ongoing Accountability

Participants receive check-ins and support as they work through the early stages of launching — so they're not building alone.

The Process

A Clear Path From Idea to Business Launch

1

Apply

Complete the interest form so we can learn about your background, goals, and business idea. There's no cost and no commitment required.

2

Discovery Call

If you may be a fit, our team schedules a virtual call to better understand your needs, goals, and eligibility for the program.

3

Build Your Plan

Accepted participants work through structured training, mentorship, and business planning with support every step of the way.

4

Launch With Support

Participants begin building their business with continued guidance, accountability, and community support during the early stages.

Our Mission

Expanding Access to Small Business Ownership

Option C Foundation is a nonprofit organization created to help people who face barriers to traditional employment pursue economic independence through entrepreneurship.

We believe people should not be permanently defined by a disability, a past mistake, or a difficult season of life. With the right structure, mentorship, and support, many people can build something of their own.

Built by Entrepreneurs Who Understand Real-World Execution

Option C Foundation was founded by Kyle K. Peterson to create a practical path for people who are often overlooked by the traditional employment system. The foundation focuses on helping participants move beyond ideas and into real business structure, planning, and action.
