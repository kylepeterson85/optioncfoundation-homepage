Our Participants

The Four Groups We Serve

Option C Foundation is built for adults who have been screened out, looked past, or written off by traditional employment — for reasons that have nothing to do with their potential. If any of the situations below describes your circumstances, your sub-page below explains how the program applies to you specifically, and how to apply.

People With Disabilities

Adults living with physical disabilities, chronic illness, mental health conditions, sensory disabilities, or other long-term health conditions that make traditional employment difficult or inaccessible. Self-employment can be built around your actual strengths, schedule, energy levels, and accommodations — without bending your life to fit someone else's job description.

Entrepreneurship Support for People With Disabilities →

People With Prior Felony Convictions

Adults with prior criminal records who are ready to move forward and build something legitimate — without facing automatic rejection in every hiring process. For many people in this situation, business ownership is one of the most direct paths to legal income, long-term stability, and reduced recidivism. Option C focuses on potential, not the past.

Business Support for People With Prior Convictions →

The Long-Term Unemployed

Adults who have been out of the workforce for an extended period — due to illness, caregiving, incarceration, or repeated rejection — and now face the impossible cycle of needing recent experience to get hired but needing a job to build experience. Building a small business breaks the loop and lets you generate real income on terms you control.

Aspiring Entrepreneurs Without Resources

Adults with a real idea, real motivation, and real potential — but without mentorship, structure, capital for an agency, or a professional network to help them launch. Option C closes that gap by pairing you with a mentor and producing the actual deliverables a small business needs to launch — at no cost.

What Every Participant Brings

What All Participants Have in Common

Our participants come from very different starting points and face very different barriers — but the program only works for people who bring three things to the table.

Motivation to Build Something

A genuine desire to create real income and real value on your own terms — not a quick fix, not a passive income fantasy, not a way to opt out of doing real work.

Willingness to Do the Work

Readiness to put in the time, focus, and follow-through that planning, learning, and launching a real small business actually requires. We do the heavy professional execution alongside you, but the ownership stays with you.

Openness to Guidance & Accountability

Willingness to be coached, to take honest feedback, and to be held to the milestones you and your mentor set together. The framework only works if the participant trusts the process enough to follow it.

Understanding the Landscape

The Barriers We Work Around

These are not character flaws or signs of inability. They are real, structural barriers that millions of capable adults face every day — and that traditional hiring systems were never designed to accommodate. Option C exists because of them.

Hiring Discrimination at Scale

Background checks, disability disclosure questions, age, employment gaps, and dozens of other filters cause capable people to be rejected before they ever get to interview — sometimes by software, before any human reads their application.

Lack of Professional Networks

Without mentors, advisors, or professional connections, launching a business can feel impossible and isolating. Option C gives every accepted participant a 1-on-1 mentor — a built-in network of one, with real business experience.

Limited Work Flexibility

Traditional employment often demands schedules, environments, and physical capacity that aren't compatible with disabilities, caregiving responsibilities, or chronic health conditions. Self-employment lets you build the business around your real life.

Knowledge & Planning Gaps

Most capable people lack the structured guidance, business plan template, or roadmap to move from idea to launch. Templates and webinars don't fix this. A mentor and a structured framework do — which is exactly what Option C provides.

Cost of Professional Services

A custom business plan, logo, website, SEO, and analytics setup from an agency typically costs thousands of dollars upfront. For most of our participants, that cost is not realistic. Option C provides all of it for free, funded by donors and grants.

The "Just Try" Trap

People are constantly told to "just start a business" — but without research, validation, structure, or accountability, most of those attempts fail in the first year. The Business Building Machine replaces guessing with a defined, sequential path.
