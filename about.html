Our Mission

Expanding Access to Small Business Ownership for People Who Need It Most

Option C Foundation exists to help adults who face barriers to traditional employment pursue economic independence through entrepreneurship. We believe that a disability, a prior conviction, or a difficult chapter of life should not permanently close every door.

We provide free business training, mentorship, planning support, and accountability to qualified participants who are serious about building a legitimate small business and a better future for themselves.

Why We Exist

The Problem We're Trying to Solve

Millions of adults in the United States face hiring barriers that have nothing to do with their potential, work ethic, or ability to build something of value. Option C was built to address this directly.

Background Checks Close Doors

People with prior felony convictions often face automatic rejection in the hiring process — regardless of how much time has passed, the nature of the offense, or the growth that has happened since. For many, the legal system has been served, but employment opportunities remain locked.

Disability Limits Traditional Employment

Traditional employment often requires schedules, environments, and physical demands that aren't compatible with many disabilities or chronic health conditions. Self-employment can offer the flexibility to build a business around a person's actual strengths, schedule, and capacity.

Long Gaps Lead to More Rejection

People who have been out of the workforce for extended periods — due to illness, incarceration, caregiving, or other circumstances — often face an impossible cycle: they can't get a job without recent experience, and they can't get experience without a job.

Ideas Without Structure Don't Launch

Many people have a viable business idea, genuine motivation, and real potential — but no mentorship, no structure, and no access to resources or guidance. Without a framework, most ideas stay ideas. Option C exists to bridge that gap.

Our Approach

We Focus on What Actually Helps People Move Forward

Option C doesn't offer generic business advice. Our program is designed specifically for people who face real barriers, and it's built to be practical, structured, and actionable.

01

Start With the Person, Not the Business

Before anything else, we take time to understand each participant's background, strengths, limitations, and goals. The right business for one person may be completely wrong for another. We start there.

02

Focus on Lawful, Realistic Businesses

We only support legitimate small business ideas that are legal, practical, and aligned with the participant's actual situation. We do not support schemes, passive income promises, or MLM-style structures.

03

Provide Structure and Accountability

One of the biggest reasons small businesses fail early is the absence of structure and outside accountability. We build both into the program through regular check-ins, planning milestones, and mentorship.

04

Prepare Participants for Independent Operation

The goal is not to create dependency on Option C — it's to help participants build the knowledge, tools, and confidence to operate their business independently over the long term.

Leadership

Built by Someone Who Understands Real-World Execution

Option C Foundation was founded by Kyle K. Peterson with a straightforward belief: people who face serious barriers to traditional employment deserve a real alternative — not platitudes, not generic advice, but a structured path toward something they can actually build and own.

Kyle built Option C to create the kind of support he wished existed for people who are motivated and capable but consistently overlooked by the traditional employment system. The foundation is focused on doing the work that actually matters: helping participants move from idea to action, and from action to a real, operating business.

The program is built on the principle that entrepreneurship, when supported with the right structure and accountability, can be a legitimate path to income and independence for people who have been told that their options are limited.

What We Believe

The Principles That Guide This Organization

People Are Not Defined by Their Circumstances

A disability, a past conviction, or a period of unemployment does not define what a person is capable of building. We evaluate potential, not just background.

Honesty and Clear Expectations

We don't overpromise. Entrepreneurship is hard. We're clear about what the program is, what it is not, and what participants will need to do to make it work.

Economic Independence Creates Dignity

Building something of your own — even something small — creates a different relationship with work, income, and self-worth. That matters beyond just the money.

Structure and Accountability Drive Results

Most people don't fail because they lack ideas. They fail without structure, without accountability, and without someone in their corner. We provide all three.

Legal and Legitimate — Always

We will only ever support lawful small business models. Our program does not accept or support participants pursuing illegal activity of any kind — full stop.

Education Over Dependence

We measure success not by how long participants stay in the program, but by how equipped they are to operate independently when they leave it.
