Program Overview

What the Option C Program Is

The Option C program is a structured entrepreneurship support program designed specifically for adults who face barriers that make traditional employment difficult or inaccessible — disability, prior felony conviction, long-term unemployment, or limited access to professional resources.

Accepted participants are paired with a mentor and walked through six structured stages of business building. Along the way, our team produces the professional deliverables a real small business needs to launch — a written business plan, a logo, a custom website, hosting, SEO, a Google Business Profile, Google Analytics, and branded social media pages. The participant is in the loop the whole way, learning how each piece works while we do the heavy lifting.

This is not a general business course. It's a hands-on implementation program where participants leave with an actual operating business, not a folder full of homework.

The Framework

Powered by the Business Building Machine

Every Option C participant is guided through the Business Building Machine — a structured, 6-stage operating system developed by Option C founder Kyle K. Peterson to take a person from "I have an idea" (or no idea at all) to "I have a real, functioning small business with customers." Each stage has a clear job, defined inputs, and concrete deliverables that must be approved before moving on. One stage at a time. No skipping. No guessing.

0

Stage 0 — Research & Direction

The job of this stage: validate the business direction with real market data before anything is built. We replace guessing with research so participants don't waste months going the wrong direction.

  • Complete an intake assessment to understand the participant's goals, skills, constraints, and any business idea they already have
  • Research the market, competitors, pricing, gaps, and customer pain points
  • Map real keyword and search demand to confirm there are people actually looking for this kind of business
  • Deliver a written Business Direction Report that the participant reviews and approves before Stage 1 begins
1

Stage 1 — Strategic Foundation

The job of this stage: give the business its identity. By the end of Stage 1, the participant can explain what the business is and who it's for in two clear sentences — and we have the foundation we need to write the full business plan.

  • Define the mission, niche, target customer, and the specific problem the business solves
  • Write the positioning — why someone chooses this business over the alternatives
  • Set a realistic 90-day goal so success is measurable, not just hopeful
  • Produce the 20+ page custom business plan covering model, market, offer, operations, financials, and the 90-day launch path
2

Stage 2 — Offer & Path

The job of this stage: turn the strategy into something a customer can actually buy. We define the offer, the price, and the path from first contact to payment so the participant has a sellable business — not just a business idea.

  • Define exactly what is being sold, what's included, and what the customer gets
  • Set pricing direction and one clear primary call-to-action
  • Map the customer journey from first contact to delivery
  • Design the logo and core brand identity around the offer — yours to keep, even if you ever leave the program
3

Stage 3 — Visibility Engine

The job of this stage: make the business visible to the people most likely to buy from it. This is the largest output stage — most of the deliverables on the homepage are produced here.

  • Build the custom website — pages written for the participant's actual audience, mobile-friendly, fast-loading
  • Apply SEO on every page — keyword targeting, meta descriptions, headings, internal linking
  • Provide free website hosting so the site stays live with no monthly bill
  • Create and verify the Google Business Profile for Maps and local 3-pack visibility
  • Install and configure Google Analytics so we can see what's working
  • Build the Facebook and Instagram business pages, branded with the logo and ready to post
4

Stage 4 — Delivery System

The job of this stage: make the business deliver consistently so growth doesn't create chaos. A business that can't reliably fulfill what it sells doesn't have a scaling problem — it has a foundation problem.

  • Document a simple, repeatable delivery process from sale to completion
  • Set up the basic systems: payments, customer communication, follow-up, and record-keeping
  • Create checklists so each customer gets the same quality experience
  • Walk participants through handling their first real customers in real time, with mentor support
5

Stage 5 — Optimize & Grow

The job of this stage: use real data to find the biggest bottleneck in the business and fix that one thing first — then plan responsibly for what comes next. We don't add complexity until the foundation is ready for it.

  • Review the numbers — visitors, leads, sales, and where the biggest drops are happening
  • Identify the single biggest bottleneck and target it directly, not everything at once
  • Provide ongoing 1-on-1 mentorship as the participant builds momentum
  • Help plan responsibly for the next phase of growth — without scaling chaos

Mentorship

How the 1-on-1 Mentorship Actually Works

Mentorship at Option C is not a single coaching session. It's a structured, ongoing relationship that runs alongside the entire Business Building Machine — so participants always have someone to bring their questions, their stuck points, and their progress to.

Matched to Your Business

You're paired with a mentor whose experience and skill set is relevant to the type of business you're building — service-based, online, local, or something else. Not random, not first-come-first-served.

Scheduled Check-Ins

Recurring 1-on-1 sessions to review progress, unstick decisions, and set the next milestone. Cadence is set with you based on the stage you're in and the pace your situation allows.

On-Demand Questions

Between sessions, you can bring questions — about a specific decision, a wording on your website, a price you're nervous about, a customer conversation that didn't go well — without waiting weeks for the next call.

Honest Feedback

If your plan has a hole, your mentor will tell you. We don't believe in motivational fluff — we believe in catching problems early so they don't break the business in month three.

Real Accountability

You and your mentor agree on what gets done between sessions. Then we hold you to it — gently but seriously. Most people don't lack ideas; they lack someone in their corner expecting them to follow through.

Built Around Your Reality

Many participants are managing a disability, a health condition, a family situation, or other constraints. We work with each person's actual capacity — not a rigid one-size-fits-all timeline.
